Measurement of Relative Water content of Wheat Leaf Under Stress Condition

To measure the Relative Water Content (RWC) of wheat leaves under stress conditions, fresh leaves were first collected and their fresh weight (FW) was recorded. The leaves were then immersed in distilled water for 4 hours to achieve full turgidity, after which the turgid weight (TW) was measured. Finally, the leaves were dried in an oven at 70°C for 48 hours to determine the dry weight (DW). RWC was calculated using the formula: RWC (%) = [(FW − DW) / (TW − DW)] × 100. This method helps assess the water retention capacity of plants under stress.

Wheat Field Soil Monitoring

Soil moisture and temperature of wheat fields were measured using a digital soil moisture meter and soil thermometer. These readings help assess root-zone conditions and plant responses under varying stress environments.s


Measuring Mungbean Leaf Using Licor6800 Portable Photosyntesis System

Leaf gas exchange parameters of mungbean were measured using the LI-COR 6800 Portable Photosynthesis System. Fully expanded, healthy leaves were selected for recording photosynthetic rate, stomatal conductance, and transpiration. This method provides insights into plant physiological responses under environmental stress conditions.

Sample Collection of Microplastics from 30 point of Turag River

Sample collection of microplastics was conducted at 30 key industrial and urban discharge points along the Turag River to assess pollution hotspots. This effort aimed to identify spatial distribution and potential sources of microplastic contamination in the river.

FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared) Analysis of Microplastics at BCSIR
